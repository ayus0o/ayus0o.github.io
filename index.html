About

I grew up between El Paso and Ciudad Juárez — two cities, one river, a life spent crossing back and forth. I never studied art formally. I learned by making things, by looking closely, by starting over.

I work on paper. Most pieces combine whatever feels right: watercolor, charcoal, ink, pastel, graphite. The material matters less than the moment I'm trying to hold onto.

I paint people I see on the bridge, strangers at bus stops, someone asleep on the metro. Faces from films. My grandmother's hands. Images that lodge in me and won't leave until I draw them.

I don't explain my work. I trust you to find what's there for you.
